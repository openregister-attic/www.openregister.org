Registers

The UK digital strategy describes registers as a national data infrastructure of authoritative lists that are held once across government.

If you’re a government service provider, you can use an API to get access to current and reliable data from registers

If you’re interested in managing a register, you can find out how to become a custodian.

The better use of data section of the government transformation strategy 2017 to 2020 includes a list of potential registers

Registers Dashboard
The shape of the data in a register is developed with a custodian, and in response to public feedback. The history page for a register documents this process.
Beta registers
Have a stable shape, and long term commitment to being kept and maintained by a custodian.
Alpha registers
May have limited content. Their shape may change as a result of working with the potential custodian and in response to wider feedback.
Discovery registers
Experimental, potential registers containing data, which may not reach alpha.
Backlog registers
Potential registers, prioritised for discovery.
